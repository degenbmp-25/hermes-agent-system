# SKILL.md — Alex Groberman: SEO for Google AI Mode

## What It Is

Alex Groberman's analysis of how Google AI Mode changes SEO content strategy. The core insight: AI Mode researches full questions, divides them into subtopics, searches each subtopic separately, discovers different supporting pages, and combines everything into one response. This creates a new standard for search visibility.

## Why It Matters

**The query fan-out effect:** One keyword page can still perform well. But one page rarely explains every part of a complicated purchase. AI Mode breaks queries into subtopics — each subtopic can attract a different supporting page.

**Traffic opportunity extends beyond broad keywords.** Each useful subtopic can attract a more specific customer with:
- A specific problem
- Likely product requirements
- A relatively focused research intent
- Potential interest in a solution

**The businesses earning the most Google AI visibility will provide useful information across the questions customers ask before making a decision.**

## Core Pattern: Query Fan-Out

```
User Question (broad)
    ↓
AI Mode researches full question
    ↓
Divides into subtopics
    ↓
Searches subtopics separately
    ↓
Discovers different supporting pages
    ↓
Combines into one response
```

## Content Requirements for AI Mode Eligibility

A page must be:
- **Indexed** and eligible to appear in Google Search with a snippet
- **Publicly accessible and crawlable**
- **Relevant** to the customer's use case
- **Genuinely useful** — not generic

### What Google Recommends

- Original and non-commodity content
- Content Google cannot easily find everywhere else

### What Gets Ignored

Publishing another generic article like "10 Benefits of Payroll Software" gives Google little reason to choose your page.

### Strong Page Content Includes

- **Original data** (e.g., payroll cost data from actual research)
- **Detailed compliance processes**
- **Comparisons based on actual testing**
- **Customer case studies**
- **Industry-specific advice**
- **Clear pricing and integration details**

## The 6-Point Visibility Standard

To rank in AI Mode, content must be:

1. **Discoverable for the category** — clear category ownership
2. **Relevant to the customer's use case** — specific to their situation
3. **Clear about pricing and features** — no hidden info
4. **Useful in comparisons** — stand out vs alternatives
5. **Supported by evidence** — data, studies, case studies
6. **Visible on authoritative third-party websites** — credible placements reinforce identity

## Third-Party Placements Matter

Groberman notes: credible third-party placements on DR50+ domains already appearing in AI search results can improve discovery while reinforcing the company's identity and category across the wider set of sources Google may encounter.

**This is not just link building — it's category positioning.**

## The "SEO Stuff" System

Groberman's system is built around one principle:

> "Provide useful information across the questions customers ask before making a decision."

Map your content to the full decision journey, not just the conversion moment.

## For HiveMind Clients

When building SEO content systems for clients:

1. **Map the decision journey** — what questions do customers ask at each stage?
2. **Create content for each subtopic** — don't try to answer everything on one page
3. **Invest in original data** — comparisons, case studies, original research
4. **Pursue third-party placements** — authoritative sites that appear in AI search
5. **Avoid generic content** — "10 Benefits of X" style articles won't rank in AI Mode
6. **Be clear about pricing/features** — AI Mode surfaces this information

## Prerequisites

- SEO content strategy work
- Client with complex purchase journey (B2B, SaaS, services)
- Ability to create original research/data

## Usage

When building SEO content strategy for a client:

1. **Map the query fan-out** — what subtopics does AI Mode see for the main query?
2. **Identify content gaps** — which subtopics lack quality coverage?
3. **Plan original content** — data, testing, case studies
4. **Build third-party placement strategy** — guest posts, partnerships
5. **Structure for discoverability** — category pages, use-case pages, comparison pages
